## PART ONE: CELL PHILOSOPHY — THE JCIM WAY

### 1.1 What Is a Cell?

A cell is the smallest expression of the church. It is not a Bible study group. It is not a prayer meeting. It is not a fellowship hangout. It is the church meeting in a home, a campus, a workplace, or online — with all the functions of the church present in miniature:

- Worship happens in the cell
- The Word is taught in the cell
- Prayer happens in the cell
- Evangelism is planned from the cell
- Pastoral care happens in the cell
- Discipleship happens in the cell
- Offering can be collected in the cell
- New converts are first nurtured in the cell

### 1.2 The JCIM Cell Conviction

"The cell is not a program OF the church — the cell IS the church in its most basic expression."

This means:
1. Every member of The Berean Church should be in a cell. No exceptions.
2. Every cell is a potential church. When a cell multiplies, it has the DNA to eventually become a campus or a church plant.
3. The pastor does not grow the church — cell leaders grow the church. The pastor equips cell leaders. Cell leaders reach people. This is Ephesians 4:11-12 in action.
4. Sunday gathers what the cells build. Without cells feeding people into Sunday, growth depends entirely on the pastor's personal magnetism and marketing. With cells, growth is organic, relational, and exponential.

### 1.3 Why People Stay in Cells (But Leave Churches)

People don't leave churches where they are known by name, missed when absent, and cared for in crisis. The Sunday service — no matter how powerful — cannot do this for every person. But a cell of 8-12 people can. The cell is the retention engine of the church.

## PART TWO: CELL STRUCTURE & TYPES

### 2.1 Cell Types

| Cell Type | Description | Location | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Home Cell | Meets in a member's home | Residential neighborhoods | Families, mixed-age groups, community-based evangelism |
| Campus Cell | Meets on a school campus | JHS, SHS, University | Students, campus evangelism, leadership development |
| Workplace Cell | Meets in an office or near a workplace | CBD, industrial areas, markets | Professionals, marketplace ministry |
| Digital Cell | Meets online via Zoom/Google Meet | Anywhere | Diaspora members, remote locations, overflow |
| Special Interest Cell | Organized around a shared life stage | Varies | Singles, young married couples, new mothers, professionals |

Starting Recommendation: Launch with Home Cells and Campus Cells first. Add Workplace and Digital cells as demand grows.

### 2.2 Cell Size Parameters

| Parameter | Standard |
|-----------|----------|
| Minimum size to form a cell | 4 people (leader + assistant + 2 members) |
| Ideal operating size | 6-12 members |
| Maximum before multiplication | 15 members |
| Multiplication trigger | When a cell consistently has 12-15 members for 3+ weeks AND an assistant leader is trained and ready |

### 2.3 Cell Leadership Structure

Every cell has exactly two leaders:

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Cell Leader | Leads the cell meeting, provides pastoral care to members, plans evangelism, reports weekly to Zone Leader, identifies and mentors the assistant leader |
| Assistant Cell Leader | Supports the cell leader, leads in the leader's absence, prepares to lead the daughter cell upon multiplication, manages attendance and follow-up |

### 2.4 Zone & District Structure (Scales As You Grow)

Phase 1 (5-10 cells): All cell leaders report directly to the General Overseer or a designated Cell Ministry Coordinator. No zones needed yet.

Phase 2 (10-20 cells): Introduce Zone Leaders. Each Zone Leader oversees 3-5 cells.

Phase 3 (20-50 cells): Introduce District Pastors. Each District Pastor oversees 3-5 zones (15-25 cells).

You don't need the full structure now. Start simple. Add layers ONLY when the span of control exceeds what one person can effectively oversee (5-7 direct reports maximum).

## PART THREE: HOW TO FORM YOUR FIRST 5 CELLS

### 3.1 Step 1: Identify Your Cell Leaders (Week 1)

Cell Leader Qualifications:
1. Born again and water baptized (or in process)
2. Completed Foundation School OR currently enrolled and showing strong commitment
3. Faithful in church attendance (rarely misses Sunday)
4. Demonstrates a servant heart and teachable spirit
5. Has a home, campus space, or workplace suitable for hosting a cell
6. Willing to commit to weekly cell meetings and weekly reporting
7. Able to lead a conversation (a facilitator, not necessarily a preacher)
8. Has a heart for people — genuinely cares about others' wellbeing

Note: At the startup phase, your leaders will not be perfect. They will be willing. You are looking for faithfulness and availability, not polish. You will train them into excellence.

### 3.2 Step 2: Identify Assistant Cell Leaders (Week 1)

For each cell leader, identify one assistant. The key requirement: willingness to learn and serve.

### 3.3 Step 3: Assign Members to Cells (Week 1-2)

Assignment Criteria:
1. Geography first — group people who live near each other
2. Campus affiliation — for campus cells, group by university or school
3. Life stage — where possible, group people with similar life contexts
4. Relationship — if someone was won to Christ by a specific member, place them in the same cell

### 3.4 Step 4: Train Your Cell Leaders (Week 2)

Hold a Cell Leaders' Training Session — a 2-3 hour intensive covering:

Module 1: The Vision (30 min) — Why cells? The biblical foundation. The JCIM cell philosophy. The 500 target and how cells get us there.

Module 2: How to Lead a Cell Meeting (45 min) — Walk through the 5W's format step by step. Practice facilitating a Word discussion. How to handle different personalities.

Module 3: Pastoral Care in the Cell (30 min) — Know your members' names, birthdays, prayer needs. Follow up on absent members within 24 hours. When to refer to the pastor.

Module 4: Evangelism from the Cell (20 min) — The "empty chair" concept. How to invite people naturally. How to welcome and integrate new people.

Module 5: Reporting & Accountability (15 min) — What to report weekly. How to submit reports. The monthly cell leaders' meeting.

Module 6: Multiplication Vision (20 min) — Every cell is pregnant with a church. The Mother-Daughter multiplication model. Target: every cell should multiply within 6-12 months.

### 3.5 Step 5: Launch the Cells (Week 3)

- All 5 cells hold their first meeting in the same week
- General Overseer visits each cell in the first 2 weeks
- First meeting combines fellowship, vision casting, and the 5W format
- After the first meeting, cell leaders report to the General Overseer

## PART FOUR: THE CELL MEETING FORMAT — THE 5W's

Total meeting time: 50-70 minutes.

### W1: WELCOME (5-7 minutes)
Purpose: Break the ice, create warmth, make everyone feel included.
- Warm greeting from cell leader
- Quick icebreaker question (rotate weekly)
- If there's a new person, welcome them warmly but don't put them on the spot

### W2: WORSHIP (8-12 minutes)
Purpose: Shift the atmosphere from social to spiritual.
- Sing 2-3 worship songs together, or play worship music and pray
- Close with a brief corporate prayer inviting God's presence

### W3: WORD (20-25 minutes)
Purpose: Engage with Scripture through facilitated discussion, NOT a sermon.

The 4-Question Method:

| Question | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| 1. What does this passage SAY? (Observation) | Get everyone reading carefully |
| 2. What does this passage MEAN? (Interpretation) | Dig into the meaning |
| 3. What does it mean to ME? (Application) | Make it personal |
| 4. What will I DO about it? (Action) | Drive transformation |

Source Material (priority order):
1. Sunday Sermon Application Guide (distributed to cell leaders by Thursday)
2. Foundation School Material
3. A pre-selected Bible passage

Facilitation Tips:
- Ask open-ended questions
- Don't answer your own questions immediately — let silence work
- Affirm every contribution
- Gently redirect off-topic discussions
- If someone dominates, invite others to share
- NEVER humiliate someone for a wrong answer

### W4: WITNESS (8-10 minutes)
Purpose: Keep evangelism central.

Part A: Testimony Time (3-5 min) — Anyone have a testimony from this week?

Part B: Evangelism Planning (5 min):
- The Empty Chair: "Who should be sitting in this chair next week?"
- Each member identifies ONE person to invite or reach out to
- The 1-1-1 Challenge: 1 prayer for a lost person, 1 invitation extended, 1 act of kindness — every week

### W5: WORKS (5-7 minutes)
Purpose: Handle logistics and close.
- Announcements
- Practical needs check ("Does anyone need help this week?")
- Attendance recorded by assistant cell leader
- Absent members noted for follow-up
- Closing prayer
- Informal fellowship time (10-15 minutes of snacks and conversation)

## PART FIVE: THE CELL MULTIPLICATION MODEL

### 5.1 The Mother-Daughter Model

When a cell reaches 12-15 members consistently and the assistant leader is trained:

ORIGINAL CELL → MOTHER CELL (6-8 members, original leader) + DAUGHTER CELL (6-7 members, former assistant as new leader)

### 5.2 How to Multiply

Step 1: Identify readiness (Month 4-6)
Step 2: Prepare the cell (2-4 weeks before) — frame it as CELEBRATION, not loss
Step 3: Commission the new cell leader
Step 4: Launch the daughter cell
Step 5: Both cells identify new assistants. Repeat.

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Thursday | Receive and prepare Cell Study Guide |
| Friday | Send meeting reminder to all members |
| Saturday | Lead the cell meeting. Take attendance. |
| Saturday Evening | Submit Weekly Cell Report |
| Sunday | Sit with cell members at church |
| Monday-Tuesday | Follow up on absent cell members |
| Midweek | Check in on members with prayer needs |

### What a Cell Leader Must NEVER Do

1. Never cancel a cell meeting without Zone Leader approval
2. Never use the cell for personal business or MLM
3. Never counsel on issues beyond your capacity — refer to the pastor
4. Never gossip about members' private matters
5. Never teach doctrine that contradicts JCIM's position
6. Never create a personality cult
7. Never collect money without authorization

## PART SEVEN: CELL HEALTH ASSESSMENT

Monthly Cell Health Scorecard (5 indicators, 3 points each = 15 max):

| Indicator | Healthy (3) | Stable (2) | At Risk (1) | Critical (0) |
|-----------|-------------|------------|-------------|---------------|
| Attendance Consistency | 80%+ weekly | 60-79% | 40-59% | Below 40% |
| New Member Integration | 2+ visitors/month | 1 visitor | Inviting but no visitors | No evangelistic activity |
| Multiplication Readiness | Assistant trained, approaching 15 | Assistant in training | No assistant identified | Leader resistant |
| Pastoral Care | All absentees followed up in 24hrs | Most followed up | Inconsistent follow-up | No follow-up |
| Spiritual Vitality | Dynamic meetings | Good but routine | Flat, declining participation | Members disengaged |

Scoring: 13-15 = Thriving | 10-12 = Healthy | 7-9 = Needs Attention | 0-6 = Critical Intervention
