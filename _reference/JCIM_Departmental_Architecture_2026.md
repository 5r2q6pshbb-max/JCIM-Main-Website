## PART ONE: THE DEPARTMENTAL ARCHITECTURE

### Organizing Principle

Not every function needs its own department. At your current stage, you need **lean, high-impact departments** that can expand as you grow. The structure below uses **7 Core Departments**, each containing **sub-units** that can eventually become standalone departments as the church scales.

Think of it like this: Christ Embassy didn't start with 40 departments. They started with a few essential ones and multiplied structure as they multiplied people. Oyedepo's Winners Chapel runs on clear departmental lanes — but those lanes were built progressively.

### DEPARTMENT 1: SECRETARIAT & ADMINISTRATION
**"The Nerve Center"**

**Scripture Foundation:** *"Write the vision, and make it plain upon tables, that he may run that readeth it."* — Habakkuk 2:2

**Why This Exists:** Nothing functions without administration. This is the backbone that holds everything together. Every report, every record, every communication flows through here.

**Department Head Title:** Chief Administrative Secretary

**Core Functions:**
- Church records and documentation management
- Correspondence (internal and external)
- Meeting coordination and minutes
- Policy documentation and compliance
- General Overseer's office support
- Inter-departmental coordination

**Sub-Units:**

**1a. Database & Information Management**
- Membership database (names, contacts, salvation dates, baptism records, cell group, department, Foundation School progress)
- Attendance tracking systems
- Visitor/first-timer records
- Digital record-keeping and backup
- Data reporting and analytics

**1b. Communications**
- Official announcements (Sunday, midweek, digital)
- Internal newsletters
- External correspondence
- WhatsApp/Telegram group management and protocols
- Bulletin and program design

**Immediate Priority Tools:**
- Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 for shared documents
- A church management software (ChurchCenter, Breeze, or a simple Google Sheets system to start)

**Minimum Staffing to Launch:** 1 Department Head + 1 Database Officer + 1 Communications Officer

### DEPARTMENT 2: FINANCE & STEWARDSHIP
**"The Treasury"**

**Scripture Foundation:** *"Moreover it is required in stewards, that a man be found faithful."* — 1 Corinthians 4:2

**Why This Exists:** The General Overseer must stop being the personal ATM of the ministry. This department creates financial systems that make the church self-sustaining, transparent, and positioned for growth.

**Department Head Title:** Financial Secretary / Chief Steward

**Core Functions:**
- Income collection and recording (tithes, offerings, first fruits, seeds, special collections)
- Expense management and disbursement
- Budget preparation and monitoring
- Financial reporting (weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- Bank account management
- Audit preparation

**Sub-Units:**

**2a. Offering & Collections Team**
- Sunday offering coordination
- Online giving setup and management
- Special project collections
- Partnership program administration

**2b. Ministry Materials & Procurement**
- Purchase of ministry supplies (communion elements, stationery, equipment)
- Inventory management
- Vendor relationships
- Asset register

**2c. Covenant Wealth & Stewardship Teaching**
- Financial literacy integration into discipleship
- Teaching series on biblical stewardship
- Testimony documentation (financial breakthroughs)

**Critical First Actions:**
1. Open a dedicated church bank account IMMEDIATELY (separate from personal funds)
2. Appoint a Financial Secretary and a Treasurer (two separate people — segregation of duties)
3. Create a simple income/expense tracking spreadsheet
4. Establish a weekly counting protocol: at least 2 people count offerings together, record, and deposit within 48 hours
5. Set a preliminary monthly budget
6. Introduce a giving culture through teaching (not just asking)

**Revenue Independence Plan:**
- Month 1-3: Transition from personal funding to congregational support
- Month 4-6: Establish regular tithe/offering baseline
- Month 7-12: Introduce partnership programs, project giving, and seed-faith campaigns
- Target: 70% congregational funding by Q3 2026, 90% by Q4 2026

**Minimum Staffing to Launch:** 1 Financial Secretary + 1 Treasurer + 2 Offering Collectors

**5b. Protocol & Guest Relations**
- VIP hosting

**5d. Décor & Ambience**
- Church interior styling
- Stage and altar design
- Seasonal decorations
- Event-specific theming

**5e. Catering & Refreshments**
- After-service refreshments (if applicable)
- Event catering coordination
- Love feast / fellowship meal planning

**5f. Health & Safety**
- First aid readiness
- Emergency response protocols

**The 7-Minute Rule (Implement Immediately):**
Every first-timer must experience within their first 7 minutes:
1. A warm greeting at the door (with a smile and a handshake)
2. Assistance finding a seat
3. A welcome pack or card placed in their hand
4. A brief orientation ("Here's what to expect today")
5. Introduction to at least one friendly member nearby

**Minimum Staffing to Launch:** 1 Protocol Director + 4 Ushers + 1 Events Coordinator + 1 Décor Lead

## PART THREE: THE DISCIPLESHIP RETENTION SYSTEM

**"We Don't Just Win Them — We Keep Them"**

This is your personal conviction, My Lord, and it must be systemized. Here is the complete discipleship pipeline:

### The JCIM Discipleship Pathway

```
STAGE 1: CONTACT
    First-time visitor → First-timer card → 48-hour follow-up
    ↓
STAGE 2: CONNECT
    Second visit → Cell group invitation → Midweek service
    ↓
STAGE 3: CULTIVATE
    Foundation School enrollment (18 Modules, Episterizo Methodology)
    ↓
STAGE 4: COMMIT
    Water baptism → Membership class → Official membership
    ↓
STAGE 5: CONTRIBUTE
    Department placement → Active service
    ↓
STAGE 6: CELL LEADERSHIP
    Cell member → Assistant cell leader → Cell leader
    ↓
STAGE 7: COMMISSION
    Zone leader → District pastor → Campus pastor → Ordained minister
```

### Tracking System

Every member should be trackable on this pipeline. At any given time, leadership should be able to answer:
- How many people are at each stage?
- Who has stalled and where?
- What is the average time from Stage 1 to Stage 4?
- What is the dropout rate between stages?

**Recommended Tool:** A simple Google Sheet or Airtable base with each member's name and their current stage, updated weekly by the Follow-Up team and Secretariat.

## PART FIVE: THE PATH TO 500 — GROWTH MATH

To reach 500 members by December 2026 (approximately 10 months), here is the growth math:

### If Starting With ~30-50 Core Members:

| Quarter | Target | Monthly Growth Needed | Primary Growth Engine |
|---------|--------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Q1 (Mar) | 80-100 | +15-20/month | Personal invitation, social media, Foundation School |

| Q4 (Dec) | 500+ | +50-60/month | Compounding cells, word-of-mouth, major events, media visibility |

### The Cell Multiplication Math:
- Start with 5 cells of 10 people = 50 people
- Each cell multiplies once every 6 months
- By Month 6: 10 cells × 12 people = 120 people in cells
- By Month 10: 20 cells × 12 people = 240 people in cells
- Plus Sunday-only attendees + new converts not yet in cells = 500+ total

**The cell system is your primary growth engine for hitting 500.** Media brings visibility. Evangelism brings first-timers. But CELLS retain and multiply.
