## PURPOSE

This document is the **heartbeat of JCIM operations.** It answers the question every leader, worker, and department head asks: **"What am I supposed to be doing today?"**

The Weekly Operational Cadence (WOC) is a repeating 7-day cycle that synchronizes every department, every leader, and every activity into a single operational rhythm. When this cadence runs consistently, the church moves from reactive ("we forgot to do that") to proactive ("it's already done because it's on the cadence").

This is how Christ Embassy runs hundreds of churches in lockstep. This is how Winners Chapel operates across continents without chaos. This is how JCIM will operate from now on.

**The rule is simple: If it's not on the cadence, it doesn't happen consistently. If it's on the cadence, it happens every week without fail.**

### Weekly Leaders' Check-In Format

**Duration:** 30-45 minutes (do NOT let this become a 2-hour meeting)

**Format:** Can be in-person, via Zoom, or via a structured WhatsApp voice note round.

**Agenda:**

| Item | Duration | Details |
|------|----------|---------|
| 1. Opening Prayer | 2 min | Brief and focused |
| 2. Sunday Review Summary | 5 min | Service Coordinator shares key numbers: attendance, first-timers, salvations, offering |
| 3. Follow-Up Report | 5 min | Follow-Up Team Lead reports: calls made, first-timer responses, absentee status, Foundation School enrollment |
| 4. Department Quick Reports | 10-15 min | Each department head gives a 1-2 minute update: wins, challenges, needs. NO long speeches. "What happened this week, what's planned for next week, what do you need?" |
| 5. Upcoming Events/Announcements | 5 min | Anything coming in the next 1-2 weeks that needs coordination |
| 6. General Overseer's Word | 5 min | Vision reinforcement, encouragement, correction if needed, priorities for the coming week |
| 7. Closing Prayer | 2 min | |

**Rule:** Every department head MUST attend or send a representative. Absence without notice is addressed.

## THE WEEKLY COMMUNICATION CASCADE

### How Information Flows Up and Down

**TOP-DOWN (Vision, Directives, Announcements)**

```
GENERAL OVERSEER
    ↓ (Thursday Leaders' Check-In or direct message)
DEPARTMENT HEADS / ZONE LEADERS
    ↓ (Thursday evening/Friday via WhatsApp group or call)
TEAM MEMBERS / CELL LEADERS
    ↓ (Cell meetings, department WhatsApp groups)
MEMBERS
```

**BOTTOM-UP (Reports, Feedback, Concerns)**

```
MEMBERS (raise concerns in cell groups or to their cell leader)
    ↑
CELL LEADERS (report to Zone Leaders via Saturday cell report)
    ↑
ZONE LEADERS (report to Cell Ministry Coordinator / General Overseer)
    ↑
DEPARTMENT HEADS (report at Thursday Leaders' Check-In)
    ↑
GENERAL OVERSEER (receives all reports, makes decisions)
```

### WhatsApp Group Structure (Recommended)

| Group | Members | Purpose |
|-------|---------|---------|
| **JCIM Leadership** | General Overseer + All Department Heads + Campus Pastors | Strategic decisions, vision communication, urgent matters |
| **Sunday Service Team** | Service Coordinator + Worship Leader + Head Usher + Media Lead + Sound Engineer + Prayer Lead | Sunday-specific coordination |
| **[Department Name] Team** | Each department has its own group | Internal department coordination |
| **Cell Leaders Network** | All cell leaders + Zone Leaders + Cell Ministry Coordinator | Cell ministry coordination, study guides, reports |
| **Church General** | All members (broadcast list, NOT group chat) | Announcements, event reminders, devotionals |

**WhatsApp Protocol:**
1. Leadership group is for LEADERSHIP matters only — no memes, no casual chat, no voice notes longer than 2 minutes
2. Urgent matters get a phone call, not a WhatsApp message
3. All official announcements go through the Secretariat/Communications sub-unit first
4. Sunday service changes are communicated through the Sunday Service Team group by Friday evening

## IMPLEMENTATION INSTRUCTIONS

### Week 1: Launch the Cadence

1. **Print this document** and distribute to every department head
2. **Hold a special leaders meeting** (not the regular Thursday check-in — a dedicated vision-casting session) where the General Overseer walks through this cadence and says: "This is how we operate from now on."
3. **Each department head acknowledges** their daily/weekly responsibilities
4. **Start the Thursday Leaders' Check-In** immediately — even if it's just 4-5 people. The rhythm matters more than the numbers.
5. **Start the reporting pipeline** immediately — even if reports are bare-bones initially. The habit matters more than the detail.

### Week 2-4: Refine and Reinforce

1. **Expect imperfection.** The first 2-3 weeks will be messy. People will forget. Reports will be late. Follow-up calls will be missed. THIS IS NORMAL.
2. **The General Overseer's role is to hold the cadence** — gently but firmly. "Did we do our Monday report? Did follow-up calls happen by Tuesday? Did cell leaders send their Saturday reports?"
3. **Celebrate compliance.** When a department hits their rhythm, acknowledge it publicly. "The Finance team has deposited every offering by Monday for three weeks straight. That's excellence."
4. **Correct non-compliance privately.** "Brother, your cell report hasn't come in the last two Saturdays. What's happening? How can I support you?"

### Month 2 Onward: The Cadence Becomes Culture

After 4-6 weeks of consistent execution, the cadence stops being a document and becomes **culture**. Leaders won't need to check the manual — they'll just know: "It's Tuesday, follow-up calls need to be done." "It's Thursday, leaders' check-in tonight." "It's Saturday, cell meeting day."

That's when you've moved from startup to **establishment.**
