## SECTION 1: THE SUNDAY SERVICE PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 Why Sunday Matters

Sunday is the **weekly harvest point** of the ministry. It is where:
- First-timers form their first impression (and decide within 7 minutes if they will return)
- The Word is preached with power and clarity
- Believers encounter the presence of God corporately
- Offerings and tithes are brought in
- Salvations and rededications happen
- The vision of the house is reinforced
- Community is built and belonging is felt

Every system in this manual serves one goal: **create an environment where the Holy Spirit is welcome, visitors feel valued, members are fed, and no one falls through the cracks.**

### 1.2 The Berean Church Sunday Culture

These are the non-negotiable cultural markers of every Berean Church Sunday:

1. **Excellence, not extravagance.** We do not need expensive equipment to run excellent services. We need preparation, attention to detail, and a spirit of excellence in every role.

2. **Warmth before worship.** The worship experience begins in the parking lot, not on the stage. The first face a visitor sees IS the church to them.

3. **The Word is central.** The sermon is not a segment — it is the reason we gather. Everything before it builds anticipation. Everything after it facilitates response.

4. **Every service is an altar call.** We never assume everyone in the room is saved. Every Sunday, the gospel is presented, and an opportunity to respond is given.

5. **Nobody leaves anonymous.** Every first-timer is identified, welcomed, and followed up. If someone attends our service and leaves without us knowing their name, we have failed.

6. **Start on time. End with purpose.** We respect people's time by starting punctually. We do not end services abruptly — we end with intentionality.

## SECTION 4: THE SERVICE FLOW / RUN SHEET

### 4.1 Standard Sunday Service Format (Approximately 2 to 2.5 hours)

This is the default run sheet. The Service Pastor may adjust segments based on the leading of the Spirit, but this is the baseline structure.

| Segment | Duration | Led By | Details |
|---------|----------|--------|---------|
| **1. Opening Prayer & Call to Worship** | 3-5 min | Service Pastor or Designated Minister | Short, powerful prayer to open. Transitions into worship. Sets the tone. |
| **2. Praise & Worship** | 25-35 min | Worship Leader & Team | Begins with high-energy praise (congregational singing, clapping, dancing). Transitions into deeper worship (intimate, reflective, Spirit-led). Worship leader must be sensitive to the atmosphere — if the Spirit is moving, DO NOT rush to the next segment. |
| **3. Prayer Segment** | 5-10 min | Service Pastor or Prayer Leader | Corporate prayer. Can be thematic (aligned with yearly theme, sermon series, or specific needs). Tongues, intercession, prophetic declarations. |
| **4. Welcome & Greetings** | 5-7 min | Service Pastor or MC | Welcome everyone, especially first-timers. Ask first-timers to stand or raise hands for recognition (NEVER embarrass them — celebrate them). "If this is your first time at The Berean Church, we are so glad you are here! We have a special gift for you." Direct them to first-timer hosts. Brief "greet your neighbor" moment (1-2 minutes max). |
| **5. Announcements** | 5-7 min | MC / Service Coordinator / Designated Person | Upcoming events, Foundation School enrollment, cell group info, special notices. Keep it concise and engaging. Use slides/screen for visual support. NEVER let announcements become a second sermon. |
| **6. Offering & Tithes** | 10-15 min | Service Pastor or Financial Secretary | Offering exhortation (brief Word-based encouragement on giving — NOT begging). Prayer over the offering. Collection by ushers. Offering song plays during collection. Online giving details displayed on screen. |
| **7. Special Ministrations** (Optional) | 5-10 min | Choir, Drama Team, Soloist, Testimony | A special song, drama presentation, dance, or a pre-arranged testimony. Not every Sunday — rotate weekly. Testimonies must be vetted and timed (3 minutes max). |
| **8. The Word — Sermon** | 35-50 min | General Overseer / Service Pastor / Guest Speaker | The main course. Solid, revelatory, doctrinally sound preaching/teaching. Scripture-centered. Application-driven. Delivered with passion and clarity. The sermon should ALWAYS include a clear takeaway that members can apply that week. |
| **9. Altar Call & Response** | 10-15 min | Service Pastor / Preacher | Every Sunday without exception. Three-layer altar call: (a) Salvation — "If you have never given your life to Christ..." (b) Rededication — "If you have walked away and want to return..." (c) Special needs — "If you need prayer for healing, breakthrough, direction..." Lead the sinner's prayer clearly. Altar workers move forward to pray with respondents. First-timer hosts identify new converts for immediate processing. |
| **10. Closing Prayer & Benediction** | 3-5 min | Service Pastor | Final prayer of blessing. Prophetic declaration over the congregation. Dismissal with clear next steps: "Join us for midweek service on [day]. If you're new, please see our welcome team at [location]. Cell groups meet this week — see your cell leader." |
| **11. Post-Service Fellowship** | Open | Hospitality Team | Background music plays. Members greet each other. First-timer hosts engage visitors. Refreshments served (if available). |

### 4.2 Service Timing Notes

- **Total Target Duration:** 2 hours to 2 hours 15 minutes
- **Hard Start:** Service begins at the published time. No exceptions. If the General Overseer is not present, the Service Pastor begins.
- **Worship Flexibility:** If the Spirit is moving powerfully in worship, the Service Coordinator communicates with the Service Pastor to adjust time from announcements or other flexible segments — NOT from the sermon or altar call.
- **Sermon Time:** The preacher should aim for 35-45 minutes. Going over 50 minutes requires exceptional anointing, not just more content.
- **Never Skip the Altar Call.** Even if the service runs long. Even if it feels repetitive. Souls are the mission.

## SECTION 5: USHERING & HOSPITALITY PROTOCOL

### 5.1 The Usher's Code

Every usher is a representative of the house. These are the non-negotiable standards:

1. **Appearance:** Neat, clean, well-groomed. Ushers wear the designated uniform or dress code. No casual or distracting attire.
2. **Attitude:** Warm, welcoming, attentive, but not overbearing. Smile. Make eye contact. Speak clearly.
3. **Positioning:** Never leave your post unless relieved. If you need to step away, signal the Head Usher.
4. **Alertness:** Scan the room constantly. Watch for visitors looking lost, empty seats that need filling, disturbances, medical emergencies, children wandering, or people needing assistance.
5. **Discretion:** Handle disruptions quietly and gracefully. Never publicly confront anyone. If someone is disruptive, approach gently and, if necessary, escort them to a private area.
6. **Phone Policy:** Personal phones are on silent mode and out of sight during service.

### 5.2 Usher Positions and Duties

**Door Ushers (Main Entrance)**
- Greet every person entering: "Good morning! Welcome to The Berean Church!"
- Open the door for people physically
- Hand first-timers a welcome card and direct them to the First-Timer Host
- Monitor who enters and exits during service
- Prevent noise from the entrance area disrupting the service

**Floor Ushers (Inside the Auditorium)**
- Guide people to available seats (walk them to the seat, don't just point)
- Manage seating to fill from front to back (never let the front rows stay empty while the back fills up)
- Collect offerings when signaled
- Assist during altar calls (guide respondents forward, provide tissues if needed)
- Monitor the auditorium for any issues

**Overflow Ushers (When Applicable)**
- Manage overflow seating areas
- Ensure overflow screens/speakers are working
- Maintain the same standard of hospitality in overflow as in the main auditorium

### 5.3 Seating Protocol

- **Front rows:** Reserved for workers arriving late from duty (NOT for VIPs sitting idle). The front should be filled first by regular members and visitors.
- **Strategy:** Ushers should actively encourage people to move forward. "There are great seats available up front — may I show you?" is better than letting the back fill while the front stays empty.
- **Visitors:** If a visitor is identified, seat them where they can see and hear well, preferably near a friendly, outgoing member.
- **Latecomers:** Seat latecomers during a worship song or natural break — NEVER during the sermon or prayer, unless there are seats immediately accessible without disruption.

## SECTION 6: FIRST-TIMER PROCESSING SYSTEM

### 6.1 Philosophy

A first-timer is the most important person in the building on any given Sunday. They represent the fruit of someone's prayer, someone's invitation, or the Holy Spirit's drawing. We do NOT let them slip through.

### 6.2 The First-Timer Journey on Sunday

**Step 1: Identification (At the Door)**
- Door ushers identify first-timers by asking: "Is this your first time with us?"
- If yes: "Welcome! We're so glad you're here. Let me introduce you to [First-Timer Host name]."
- Hand them a **First-Timer Welcome Card** (see template in Section 15)

**Step 2: Welcome & Seating (First-Timer Host)**
- The First-Timer Host warmly receives them
- Gives them a brief orientation: "Here's what to expect today — we'll have worship, the Word, and a time of prayer. Just relax and enjoy the service."
- Walks them to a good seat
- Introduces them to a friendly member seated nearby: "This is [Name], they'll take care of you."
- Collects the completed Welcome Card before the service starts or during the offering

**Step 3: In-Service Recognition (Welcome Segment)**
- During the Welcome segment, the Service Pastor acknowledges first-timers: "If this is your first time, please stand so we can welcome you!" (or raise your hand — choose the less intimidating option for your culture)
- Congregation applauds and welcomes them
- This must feel CELEBRATORY, not interrogative

**Step 4: Gift & Connection (Post-Service)**
- After the service, the First-Timer Host finds every identified first-timer
- Gives them a **Welcome Gift** (this can be simple: a booklet by the General Overseer, a church brochure, a pen with the church logo — whatever is available)
- Takes a photo with them (for the database and social media — with permission)
- Confirms contact details on the Welcome Card are complete
- Invites them to stay for fellowship or refreshments
- Says: "We'd love to see you again next Sunday. Can we add you to our WhatsApp group for updates?"

**Step 5: Data Entry (Same Day)**
- Secretariat / Database team enters all first-timer data into the church database THAT SAME DAY
- Data captured: Full name, phone number, email, address, how they heard about the church, who invited them (if anyone), prayer requests

**Step 6: The 48-Hour Follow-Up (Monday-Tuesday)**
- Follow-Up team calls or messages EVERY first-timer within 48 hours
- Script: "Hello [Name]! This is [Your Name] from The Berean Church. We are so glad you joined us on Sunday! How was your experience? We'd love to have you back this [midweek service day] or next Sunday. Is there anything we can pray with you about?"
- Log the call outcome in the database: Reached / Not reached / Interested / Not interested

### 6.3 First-Timer Tracking Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| First-timers identified per Sunday | Track and report weekly |
| Welcome cards completed | 100% of identified first-timers |
| 48-hour follow-up completed | 100% of first-timers contacted |
| Second-visit rate | 40%+ of first-timers return within 4 weeks |
| Foundation School enrollment from first-timers | 25%+ within 8 weeks |

## SECTION 7: OFFERING & FINANCIAL COLLECTION PROTOCOL

### 7.1 The Offering Exhortation

The offering time is NOT fundraising. It is a **worship moment** where members express gratitude and faith through giving. The exhortation should be:

- Brief (3-5 minutes maximum)
- Scripture-based (teach a giving principle, share a covenant promise)
- Faith-building (stir expectation, not guilt)
- Clear about what is being collected (tithe, offering, special projects, etc.)
- NEVER manipulative, coercive, or shaming

**Example Structure for Offering Exhortation:**
1. Read a Scripture on giving or stewardship (30 seconds)
2. Brief exposition or encouragement (2-3 minutes)
3. Pray over the offering (1 minute)
4. Release the ushers to collect

### 7.2 Collection Process

1. Service Pastor/MC says: "Ushers, please come forward for the offering."
2. Ushers take offering baskets and move to designated starting positions
3. Background worship music or offering song plays
4. Ushers walk row by row, holding the basket steady for each person
5. Online giving details displayed on screen simultaneously: "You can also give via [Mobile Money number / App / Bank transfer]"
6. Offering envelopes available for those who want to designate (tithe, offering, building fund, missions, etc.)
7. After all rows are covered, ushers bring baskets to the front / designated collection point
8. Service Pastor or MC prays a prayer of blessing over the givers and the offering
9. Two designated offering counters immediately take the collection to a secure, private room for counting

### 7.3 Offering Counting Protocol (Happens Immediately After Collection)

**Rule: NEVER less than 2 people counting. NEVER 1 person alone with the money.**

1. Two appointed counters (approved by the Financial Secretary) go to the counting room
2. Door is closed but not locked (transparency)
3. All cash is counted twice independently and amounts compared
4. All envelopes are opened and contents recorded with the donor's name (for tithe receipts)
5. Mobile money and online transfers are reconciled with the platform records
6. A **Sunday Offering Report** is completed (see template in Section 15):
   - Total cash collected
   - Total mobile money / transfers
   - Breakdown by category (tithe, offering, special, etc.)
   - Number of givers (for tracking trends)
7. Both counters sign the report
8. Cash is secured in a lockbox or safe
9. Bank deposit made within 48 hours (Monday or Tuesday at the latest)
10. Report submitted to Financial Secretary same day

### 7.4 Types of Offerings

| Type | Frequency | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Tithe** | Weekly/Monthly | 10% of income, brought by members as covenant practice |
| **General Offering** | Every service | Freewill offering from anyone present |
| **First Fruits** | Monthly/Annually | First income of the month or year, given in gratitude |
| **Seed Faith / Special Seed** | As led | Sacrificial giving based on specific faith expectations |
| **Building/Project Fund** | As announced | Designated giving toward capital projects |
| **Missions Offering** | Monthly | Specifically for missions and church planting |
| **Welfare/Benevolence** | As needed | Giving toward member welfare and community support |
| **Thanksgiving Offering** | Monthly Thanksgiving Service | Special giving in gratitude for God's faithfulness |

## SECTION 8: ALTAR CALL & MINISTRY TIME PROTOCOL

### 8.1 The Altar Call Structure

Every Sunday altar call follows this three-layer structure:

**Layer 1: Salvation (Always First, Always Given)**
- "If you are here today and you have never made Jesus Christ the Lord of your life — or if you're not sure where you stand with God — this is your moment. I'm going to ask you to step forward, and we are going to pray together."
- Wait. Give time. Don't rush.
- Lead the sinner's prayer CLEARLY and SLOWLY — as if the person has never heard these words before:
  - "Lord Jesus, I believe You are the Son of God. I believe You died for my sins and rose again. I confess that I am a sinner, and I ask You to forgive me. I receive You today as my Lord and Savior. I am born again. I am a child of God. In Jesus' name, Amen."
- Celebrate: "Heaven is rejoicing! We welcome you into the family of God!"

**Layer 2: Rededication (After Salvation)**
- "If you once walked with God but you've drifted — if you know you need to come back — step forward now. There is no shame. The Father is waiting with open arms."
- Pray a prayer of restoration and recommitment

**Layer 3: Special Needs (After Rededication)**
- "If you need prayer for healing, for a breakthrough, for direction, for your family, for your finances — come forward and our prayer team will minister to you."
- Altar workers move to the front
- Altar workers pray individually with each respondent (laying on of hands, as led by the Spirit)
- The preacher or Service Pastor may minister to specific needs as the Spirit leads

### 8.2 Altar Worker Guidelines

1. **Only trained, approved altar workers** may minister at the altar. Not every enthusiastic member — only those who have been trained.
2. **Same-gender ministry:** Male altar workers pray with men, female with women. If this is not possible, always have a second person present.
3. **Listen before you pray.** Ask the person briefly: "What would you like prayer for?" Then pray specifically.
4. **No long sermons at the altar.** Pray with faith, with authority, and with compassion — but keep it focused.
5. **Document new converts:** Every person who comes forward for salvation must be documented. Get their name, phone number, and who invited them. Hand this to the First-Timer Host or Follow-Up team immediately.
6. **Refer complex issues:** If someone shares something beyond prayer (mental health crisis, abuse, severe depression), pray with them and then refer them to the Pastoral Care Coordinator. Do not attempt to counsel at the altar.

### 8.3 New Convert Processing (Immediately After Altar Call)

1. New converts are guided to a designated area (a "Decision Corner" or "Welcome Area")
2. A Follow-Up team member sits with them
3. Collects: Full name, phone number, email, address
4. Gives them a New Believer's pack (if available): a simple tract, a Gospel of John, or a welcome booklet
5. Explains next steps: "We are so excited about your decision! Here's what happens next — we'll call you this week, and we'd love to invite you to our Foundation School where you'll learn more about your new life in Christ."
6. Takes a photo (with permission) for the church records
7. Enters data into the database THAT SAME DAY
8. 48-hour follow-up call happens (see Section 6)

## SECTION 9: COMMUNION SERVICE PROTOCOL (Monthly)

### 9.1 Frequency and Scheduling

- Communion is observed on the **first Sunday of every month** (unless moved by the General Overseer)
- It is integrated into the regular Sunday service, typically after the sermon or during the ministry time segment

### 9.2 Preparation

| Task | Responsible | Details |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Purchase communion elements | Ministry Materials / Finance | Bread (unleavened if possible, or crackers) and grape juice (non-alcoholic) |
| Prepare communion trays | Hospitality / Designated team | Bread broken into small pieces, juice poured into individual cups. Trays covered with clean white cloth. |
| Prepare communion table | Décor / Ushers | Clean white cloth on the communion table, elements arranged neatly, candles (optional) |
| Prepare extra elements | Hospitality | Always prepare 20% more than expected attendance |

### 9.3 Communion Service Flow

1. **Introduction and Teaching** (5-7 min) — Service Pastor gives a brief teaching on the significance of communion, rooted in 1 Corinthians 11:23-26 and the Gospels
2. **Self-Examination Moment** (2-3 min) — "Let a man examine himself, and so let him eat of that bread, and drink of that cup." A moment of silence and personal reflection. Soft worship playing.
3. **The Bread** — Service Pastor takes the bread, gives thanks, breaks it. "This is My body, which is broken for you. Do this in remembrance of Me." Ushers distribute. All partake together on the pastor's instruction.
4. **The Cup** — Service Pastor takes the cup, gives thanks. "This cup is the new covenant in My blood. As often as you drink it, do this in remembrance of Me." Ushers distribute. All partake together.
5. **Closing Prayer** — Prayer of thanksgiving, declaration of the covenant benefits, and blessing.
6. **Ushers collect used cups** — Discreetly and efficiently after the prayer.

### 9.4 Communion Notes

- Children who have not made a personal decision for Christ are gently excused or given a blessing instead
- First-timers who are not believers are informed: "Communion is for those who have received Jesus as Lord. If you haven't yet, this is a beautiful moment to observe, and when you make that decision, you'll partake with us!"
- Anyone who came forward for salvation earlier in the service IS eligible to partake — they are now believers

## SECTION 11: CHILDREN'S CHURCH OPERATIONS

### 11.1 Philosophy

Children are not a distraction to be managed — they are disciples to be formed. Children's Church runs SIMULTANEOUSLY with the adult service and must be treated with the same level of preparation, excellence, and intentionality.

### 11.2 Age Groups

| Group | Age Range | Teacher Ratio |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Nursery/Toddlers | 0-3 years | 1 adult per 3-4 children |
| Little Bereans | 4-6 years | 1 adult per 5-6 children |
| Junior Bereans | 7-9 years | 1 adult per 8-10 children |
| Pre-Teens | 10-12 years | 1 adult per 10-12 children |

(Adjust groups based on your actual children's population. You may start with just 2 groups and expand.)

### 11.3 Children's Church Format (60-75 minutes)

| Segment | Duration | Details |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Welcome & Icebreaker | 5 min | Fun activity, greeting, roll call |
| Praise & Worship | 10-15 min | Age-appropriate songs, actions, dancing |
| Memory Verse | 5 min | Weekly verse with repetition and fun recitation |
| Bible Story / Lesson | 15-20 min | Interactive teaching (visuals, props, storytelling) |
| Activity / Craft | 10-15 min | Coloring, craft, worksheet related to the lesson |
| Prayer | 5 min | Simple, child-led prayers with guidance |
| Snack & Dismissal | 5-10 min | Light refreshment, transition back to parents |

### 11.4 Safety Protocols

1. **Two-adult rule:** Never have a single adult alone with children. Always a minimum of 2 adults in the room.
2. **Check-in/Check-out:** Parents must sign children in at the beginning and out at the end. Use a simple numbering or tag system.
3. **No unauthorized adults:** Only approved, vetted Children's Ministry workers are in the children's area during service.
4. **First aid kit:** Available in the children's area at all times.
5. **Allergies:** Parents must declare any food allergies at check-in. Snacks should be allergy-aware.
6. **Incident reporting:** Any injury, behavioral issue, or unusual incident must be documented and reported to the Children's Ministry Leader and a parent immediately.

## SECTION 12: ONLINE/STREAMING SERVICE PROTOCOL

### 12.1 Why This Matters

Your online audience is not a secondary audience. In the Christ Embassy model, the online congregation IS a full expression of the church. Every online viewer is a potential member, a potential first-timer, a potential soul. Treat the livestream with the same seriousness as the physical service.

### 12.2 Technical Setup

| Component | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Camera | Minimum: 1 good smartphone on a tripod. Ideal: dedicated camera with HDMI output |
| Internet | Stable connection. Minimum 10 Mbps upload speed. Wired connection preferred over WiFi. |
| Streaming Platform | YouTube Live, Facebook Live, or both simultaneously (use Restream or similar for multi-platform) |
| Audio Feed | Direct feed from the sound console to the streaming device — NOT ambient room audio from a microphone |
| Graphics/Lower Thirds | Display pastor's name, Scripture references, church info, giving details on screen |
| Monitoring | One person dedicated to monitoring the stream quality and chat |

### 12.3 Online Engagement Protocol

1. **Welcome online viewers** during the Welcome segment: "To everyone joining us online — from Ghana, from the USA, from wherever you are — welcome to The Berean Church!"
2. **Dedicated online host** monitors the chat, responds to comments, and identifies online first-timers
3. **Online altar call:** "If you're watching online and you want to give your life to Christ, type 'I GIVE MY LIFE TO JESUS' in the chat right now." Online host captures these names.
4. **Online giving instructions** displayed on screen during offering
5. **Post-service:** Online host sends a follow-up message to all identified online first-timers and new converts

### 12.4 Content After the Livestream

Within 24-48 hours after Sunday:
1. Full sermon uploaded to YouTube (edited, with intro/outro)
2. Audio version uploaded to podcast platform
3. 3-5 short clips extracted for social media
4. 5-10 quote graphics designed and scheduled
5. Sermon summary posted on website/blog

## SECTION 13: EMERGENCY & CONTINGENCY PROTOCOLS

### 13.1 Medical Emergency

1. Usher or nearest worker calls for the Health/Safety officer
2. If someone collapses: Do not crowd them. Create space. Check responsiveness.
3. Call emergency services if needed (Ghana: 112 or 193 for ambulance)
4. Designate someone to meet the ambulance at the entrance
5. Service continues if possible (the congregation does not need to panic)
6. Service Pastor may pause to pray briefly for the individual
7. Incident documented after service

### 13.2 Power Outage

1. Service continues. The Word does not need electricity.
2. If worship was in progress, transition to acoustic/a cappella worship
3. Ushers ensure safety (watch for tripping hazards, calm any anxiety)
4. If backup generator is available, facility team activates it
5. If outage is prolonged and venue becomes uncomfortable, Service Pastor may adjust the service length

### 13.3 Disruptive Individual

1. Nearest usher approaches calmly and discreetly
2. Speak gently: "I can see you're upset. Can we step outside for a moment so I can help you?"
3. If the person resists, a second usher joins. Remain calm. Do not engage physically unless there is a safety threat.
4. If the situation escalates, Head Usher contacts the Service Coordinator, who may pause the service briefly or have the Service Pastor address the room with a calming word.
5. Incident documented after service.

### 13.4 Fire or Building Threat

1. Nearest worker alerts the Head Usher and Service Coordinator immediately
2. Service Pastor calmly instructs the congregation to evacuate: "We need everyone to exit calmly through the doors. Ushers will guide you."
3. Ushers direct people to exits — DO NOT allow stampeding
4. Children's Church leaders ensure all children are evacuated and accounted for
5. Assembly point: designated area outside the building
6. Head count taken at assembly point
7. Emergency services called (Ghana: 112 or 192 for fire)

## SECTION 14: WEEKLY SERVICE REVIEW PROCESS

### 14.1 The Monday Review (Within 24 Hours)

Every Monday, the Service Coordinator compiles the **Sunday Service Report** and sends it to the General Overseer / Campus Pastor and all department heads. This report includes:

1. **Attendance:** Total physical attendance, online viewers, children's attendance
2. **First-Timers:** Number identified, names, contact collected (Y/N)
3. **Salvations/Rededications:** Number and names of new converts
4. **Offering Total:** Amount collected (cash + digital), compared to previous week
5. **Service Flow Notes:** What went well, what didn't, any timing issues
6. **Technical Issues:** Sound problems, media glitches, streaming issues
7. **Incidents:** Any medical, safety, or behavioral incidents
8. **Action Items:** What needs to be fixed before next Sunday

### 14.2 The Wednesday Debrief (Optional but Recommended)

A 15-30 minute meeting or WhatsApp voice note from the Service Coordinator to department heads addressing:
- Follow-up status on first-timers (have all been contacted?)
- Any unresolved issues from Sunday
- Preparation status for the coming Sunday
- Any changes to next Sunday's run sheet

### 14.3 Monthly Service Review (First Leaders Meeting of the Month)

At the monthly all-leaders meeting, review:
- Average attendance trend (growing, plateauing, declining?)
- First-timer retention rate (how many returned within 4 weeks?)
- Giving trends (increasing, stable, declining?)
- Departmental performance (which departments are executing well, which need support?)
- Service quality feedback (from leaders and trusted members)
- Set targets for the next month
